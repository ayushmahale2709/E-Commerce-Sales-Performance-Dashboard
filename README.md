# E-Commerce Sales Performance Dashboard (Power BI)

## Project Overview

This project presents an interactive E-Commerce Sales Performance Dashboard built using Power BI. The dashboard is designed to help stakeholders understand revenue distribution, customer purchasing behavior, and product performance through clear and actionable insights.

The focus is not just on visualizing data, but on telling a business story that supports smarter decision-making.

## Business Objective

The primary objective of this dashboard is to answer key business questions such as:

- Which regions are driving the highest revenue?
- How does revenue change across months?
- Which products contribute the most to sales?
- Who are the top revenue-generating customers?
- How frequently are customers purchasing?

## Key Features

### Revenue by Region (Drill-Down Enabled)

- Donut chart showing revenue contribution across regions (North, West, South, East)
- **Drill-down functionality implemented:**
  - Region → Product → Customer
- Allows users to explore deeper levels of data directly from the chart
- Helps identify not just top regions, but also what products and customers are driving that revenue

---

### Monthly Revenue Trend

- Line chart displaying revenue across all months
- Highlights:
  - Peak sales in **Q4 (Oct–Nov)**
  - Dip around **mid-year (June)**
- Useful for identifying seasonality and planning strategies

## Insights

- Revenue is heavily concentrated in the **North and West regions (~57%)**, indicating strong performance in these areas, while South and East regions present clear opportunities for expansion.

- The monthly revenue trend shows a **clear seasonal pattern**, with peaks in Q4 (October–November) and a dip around June, suggesting the need for mid-year promotional strategies.

- The **top 3 products (Mixer, Shoes, Phone)** contribute a significant portion of total revenue, indicating uneven product performance and opportunities to improve lower-performing items.

- The **average orders per customer (3.16)** indicates moderate repeat purchase behavior, highlighting potential to increase retention through loyalty programs and targeted campaigns.

- A small group of customers contributes a large share of revenue, revealing a **customer concentration risk**, where losing key customers could impact business performance.

- The relatively high **revenue per customer (₹26.09K)** suggests a valuable customer base, making retention strategies more important than aggressive acquisition.
